CheckLists & Scoop of Work
Daily Inspections
  • Response to any maintenance requests or emergencies.
  • Walkthrough throughout the building to identify any issues or concerns.
  • Lighting and paver check throughout the property.
  • Physically inspect the chillers plant.
  • Maintain accurate records of maintenance activities, including work orders, inspections, and repairs.
  • Daily Blowing the outsides of the property, including the loading dock. Ensuring a clean and well-maintained appearance.
  • Check with current contractors and vendors to ensure that tasks are progressing according to schedule and meeting expectations.
  • Monitor Fire Panel at least once a day to look for new troubles or supervisory to identify and resolve issues quickly.
  • Oversee irrigation systems on a daily basis.
  • Check Building Automation System for Alarms or Alerts.
  • Respond to retail issues related to the building, such as leaks and electrical problems.
Weekly Inspections
  • Inspect and maintain generators rooms.
  • Inspect and maintain elevators.
  • Inspect and maintain escalators.
  • Inspect and address electrical circuit problems.
  • Inspect landscape to report to vendors any issues.
  • Check and adjust automation in Lighting Systems and Irrigation Sprinklers.
  • Inspect HVAC equipment and systems.
  • Physically inspect the chillers for new alarms, troubles, or unusual behaviors.
  • Flush water through p-traps to prevent the buildup of insects and unpleasant smells.
  • Inspect and maintain security systems, e.g., doors and locks.
  • Inspect underground tanks.
  • Generator fuel current capacity check.
Monthly Inspections
  • Adjust lighting as needed to implement energy-saving measures.
  • Test and maintain emergency lighting and exit signs.
  • Conduct water tests for underground tanks.
  • Test and maintain backup batteries for emergency systems.
  • Test and inspect building management systems (BMS).
  • Review the status of pest control inspections and treatments with vendors.
  • Generator testing and maintenance.
  • Walking underrail area to identify issues.
  • Performing water meter readings for all retails and residential spaces (23 meters).
  • Performing electrical meter readings for all retails and residential spaces.
  • Reprogram irrigation system to ensure efficient water usage and proper irrigation of the landscape.
Quarterly Inspections
  • Roof Inspection for signs of damage or wear and tear, e.g., leaks and cracks.
  • Electrical System Inspection: inspect every electrical room in the building for signs of wear and tear.
  • HVAC System inspection for damage or wear and tear. Including all components: Ductwork, filters, and thermostats.
  • Check and schedule building inspections such as 1-year fire system inspection.
  • Emergency Preparedness Inspection. Evacuation plan and building codes.
  • Chiller inspection with vendor (Trane).
  • Fire sprinkler inspection with vendor (City Fire).
  • Chiller plant water treatment with vendor (Advance Water).
  • Visual inspection of elevator and escalator components.
  • Check for any annual inspections to coordinate with vendors.
